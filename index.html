About me

Just your everyday humble private citizen, treading through the idiosyncrasies of life. I have an affinity for computers, summers in Asia, pets, The Simpsons, all-you-can-eat, video games, weightlifting, biking, and hiking-- among other things.

I graduated with a B.S. in Computer Science @ UC Davis in June 2016, pursuing a meaningful career in the world of IT. Currently working as a system administrator, I hope to make an impact by being dependable and approachable. I also wear hats in help desk, systems engineering, QA, and a bit of DevOps. I'm eager to learn.

What I've done

Home Lab

Network Diagram
Snapshot
Home Network Diagram

I set up a rather unnecessarily complex home network that takes advantage of pfSense for routing and firewall, with the goal of isolating networks for LAN, IoT, game, and security camera devices. I'd say that's the spirit of homelab, yeah?

pfSense is running on an old laptop in a router-on-a-stick configuration. My ESXi host is running services such as piHole, FreeNAS (2x3TB RAID1), Jellyfin, and Minecraft and Valheim game servers. More than satisfied with this cheapy 32GB host.
